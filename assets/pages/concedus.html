    Frequently Asked Questions

    1. What is a Hardware Wallet?

    A hardware wallet is a secure physical device used to store your private keys offline, protecting your assets from online threats. It connects to devices for transactions and requires physical confirmation, providing enhanced security compared to software wallets. Concedus is a cold wallet from Concedus Electronics.

    2. How to transfer my crypto assets to the Concedus?

    To transfer assets to Concedus, download the Concedus Wallet app, pair it with your Concedus via Bluetooth, and set up a new wallet with a securely generated 24-seed phrase. Then, simply send your assets to the address of your new wallet, now protected by Concedus.

    3. What is the website for firmware updates?

    Keep your Concedus wallet up to date with the latest firmware. Regular updates ensure enhanced features and security, giving you peace of mind as you manage your crypto assets with the latest advancements.

    4. What is FIDO U2F used for?

    The FIDO U2F feature on Concedus adds a robust second layer of authentication for your online accounts. By using Concedus as a physical second-factor tool, even if your credentials are compromised, unauthorized access is blocked without the device being physically present. Its a highly effective and simple way to enhance your account security.

    Concedus Wallet is a hardware wallet designed solely for storing crypto, NFTs, and digital assets. It does not support buying, selling, or trading.
